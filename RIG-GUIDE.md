## How to read a spot — the two forces that concentrate fish

Before the list, two principles drive *why* a spot fires. SendIt tags spots by these.

### 🔥 Hot water (thermal refuges) — the winter edge
Fish are cold-blooded; in winter they seek the warmest water they can find, because warmth = metabolism = feeding. Two thermal sources matter on this coast:

1. **Power-station warm-water outlets.** The Central Coast / Lake Macquarie coal stations pump huge volumes of heated cooling water back into the lakes. <cite index="2-104,2-105">Fish are attracted to the hot water that power plants release into the lake</cite>, and <cite index="2-247">during winter, warm water discharge created by power stations attracts large quantities of juvenile fish</cite>. The temperature lift is dramatic — <cite index="2-65">Vales Point's thermal discharge has been measured up to 11.3°C above ambient lake temperature in winter and 15.7°C above ambient in summer</cite>.
   - **⚠️ CRITICAL LEGAL CLOSURE:** the discharge canals themselves are off-limits much of the year. <cite index="2-244">The waters in and adjacent to the outlet canals of Munmorah, Eraring and Vales Point power stations are closed to all methods of fishing between 6 pm and 6 am from 1 May to 31 August annually</cite>, and <cite index="2-245">individuals can face maximum penalties of up to $22,000 and/or six months imprisonment for violating these closures</cite>. Fish the *open lake near* the warm influence, not the closed canals — and check current signage.
   - **⚠️ Sunsetting:** these outlets are disappearing. <cite index="2-72">Munmorah power station was decommissioned in 2012</cite>, and the University of Newcastle warns the lake's fish will face change as <cite index="2-104">the Eraring and Vales Point coal-fired power stations shut down</cite> (Eraring ~2025, Vales Point ~2029). Treat the thermal edge as a *bonus while it lasts*, not a permanent feature.

2. **Natural thermal mass.** Big shallow estuaries hold warmth. <cite index="4-13">Lake Macquarie runs 1–2 degrees warmer than the ocean, which keeps summer species like flathead and squid active longer</cite> — a milder, legal, year-round version of the same edge.

### 🌊 Pressure water (current & structure) — the year-round edge
"Pressure water" is moving, squeezed water — channel mouths, bridge pylons, training walls, headlands and tide races. Current concentrates bait and forces predators to feed in predictable lanes.
- **Channel constrictions** are gold: <cite index="4-4,4-5">the single narrow Swansea Channel lets ocean species push through on the tide while resident fish thrive in the warm, food-rich shallows — making it one of the best fishing spots on the NSW coast</cite>, where <cite index="4-6,4-7,4-8">kingfish station in the current to ambush bait, mulloway hold in the deeper holes, and salmon and trevally push through on the tide changes</cite>.
- **Bridges & tide races:** the Rip Bridge and The Entrance channel pack strong tidal flow that funnels fish — fish the **tide changes**, not slack water.
- **Barometric pressure** matters too: a **falling barometer** (front approaching) typically fires the bite; very high, rising, "bluebird" high-pressure days slow it. SendIt already weights this in the score.

## The corridor, region by region

> **v1.3 added the southern half of the coast** — the five sections below (Illawarra → Parramatta River → Eastern Beaches) extend the original Sydney→Newcastle research south and inland. The Sydney Harbour → Newcastle sections follow.

### South A) Illawarra & South Coast (Wollongong → Gerroa)
The "Gong" and south is a salmon, bream, flathead and snapper coast. Grounded in Localsearch, Almac Trailers, Fishbrain, Destination Kiama and r/wollongong.
- **Lake Illawarra** (33 km²) — bream, whiting, flathead, flounder; the **Windang channel/bridge** runs whiting & bream in summer, luderick in winter, with quick tides (fish ~30 min either side of the change).
- **Bellambi Point** — rocks for bream/tailor/trevally; salmon, drummer, luderick & kingfish off the point.
- **Perkins Beach (Towradgi)** — an **artificial reef (2019)** makes it an abundant hotspot: bream, salmon, flathead, tailor, jewfish.
- **Bass Point (Shellharbour)** — renowned reef/headland for **snapper and kingfish** (experienced rock anglers only).
- **Minnamurra River** — top species dusky flathead, white trevally, bream. **Kiama Harbour / Blowhole Point** — salmon, bonito, kings, snapper. **Werri (Gerringong)** & **Seven Mile (Gerroa)** — surf salmon, tailor, jew, whiting.
- Also: Towradgi, Wollongong Harbour (Belmore Basin), Hill 60/Port Kembla, Austinmer, Windang Beach.

### South B) Royal National Park & Port Hacking (incl. Bundeena)
A bream/flathead estuary system fringed by Royal NP beaches and rock. Grounded in Sutherland Shire, the Bundeena Guide and Fishbrain.
- **Bundeena Bay** — dusky flathead, bream, salmon, whiting, squid; surf-cast off the bay. **Maianbar** sand flats for flathead/whiting.
- **Hacking River** — Australian **bass** in the fresh up around Audley, bream & flathead lower (e.g. **Swallow Rock Reserve, Grays Point**, with a deep channel by the ramp).
- **Royal NP beaches/rock** — **Wattamolla** (salmon, bream, drummer), **Garie** & **Jibbon** (salmon, tailor, whiting). **Gunnamatta Bay (Cronulla)** fishes around the baths (nets concentrate bait). **Cronulla Beach / Bate Bay** — salmon, mulloway, bream, whiting.

### South C) Botany Bay & Georges River
Bream, flathead, whiting and jew, with bridges as the prime *pressure water*. Grounded in Georges Riverkeeper and FishAngler.
- The river's classic land-based marks are the **bridges**: **Captain Cook**, **Tom Uglys** and **Como** — pylons & current hold bream, flathead, luderick and school jew on the tide.
- **La Perouse** (Botany Bay north head) — kings & salmon off the rocks, bream & squid in the bay. **Silver Beach (Kurnell)** & **Dolls Point** — whiting, flathead, bream on the flats.
- **Woronora River** — bass up top, bream & flathead lower. **Kogarah Bay**, **Sans Souci** — bream & flathead around moorings and rock walls.

### South D) Parramatta River (release-only — dioxin advisory)
Big bream, flathead, jew and even kingfish in the upper harbour — but **catch-and-release only west of the Sydney Harbour Bridge** due to a dioxin dietary advisory. Grounded in At Parramatta and r/FishingAustralia.
- **Meadowbank / Kissing Point Wharf** — big bream, flathead & whiting, best after dark; **closed to fishing 5–10am**.
- **Huntleys Point Wharf (Gladesville)** — deep wharf for jew, kings & bream after 8pm. **Clarkes Point Reserve (Woolwich)** — breakwall into deep water for luderick, bream, mulloway and live-bait kings/snapper (east side run-out, south side run-in).
- Also: **Iron Cove Bridge**, **Rodd Point**, **Cabarita Park**, plus the snaggy **Lane Cove River**.

### South E) Eastern Beaches (Bondi → Malabar)
Surf and rock between the heads. Grounded in FishAngler, DECKEE/Fishraider and local guides.
- **Maroubra** (and **Magic Point**) — salmon & big jew off the beach, kings/salmon/bonito off the deep rock platform (dangerous in swell). **Bondi**, **Coogee**, **Clovelly** (luderick on weed), **Malabar/Long Bay** (sheltered bream & whiting).

### 1) Sydney Harbour & beaches (the start line)
Sydney Harbour is a world-class shore fishery. <cite index="1-178">It offers quality fishing for dusky flathead, mulloway, bream, luderick, mullet, tarwhine, silver trevally, squid and leatherjacket</cite>, and <cite index="1-179">in the warmer months it's invaded by pelagics including Australian salmon, bonito, frigate mackerel and kingfish</cite>. <cite index="1-181">More than 120 known land-based locations exist around the Harbour.</cite>

| Spot | Type | Target species | Why it fires |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Camp Cove (Watsons Bay)** | Beach + rock point | Kingfish, squid | <cite index="1-41,1-42">The rocky point at the southern end can produce a really big kingfish — Sydney Harbour's best shore-based access to quality kingfish</cite>, with squid at your feet for bait. |
| **Middle Head** | Rock platform | Kingfish, tailor, drummer | <cite index="1-5,1-8">Holds kings where the reef hits the sand on the northern end, and is a great rock spot for kings, tailor and drummer</cite> — but exposed to ocean swell. |
| **Clifton Gardens / Chowder Bay** | Jetty + rocks | Kingfish, jewfish | <cite index="1-15,1-17">The jetty drops into deep water and produces kings and jewfish; the drop-off to midstream is a renowned night jew spot.</cite> |
| **Rose Bay (the "Blue Hole")** | Sand flat | Flathead, whiting | <cite index="1-33,1-34">The sand flat and the Blue Hole are great flathead and whiting ground — you can wade the flats at low tide to the edge of the hole.</cite> |
| **Bradleys Head** | Rock | Bream, luderick, flathead, salmon, tailor | <cite index="1-21">You can pick up bream, luderick, flatties, salmon and tailor</cite> (busy boat traffic — care). |
| **Open ocean beaches** (Curl Curl, Dee Why, Long Reef, Narrabeen, Palm Beach) | Surf gutters | Whiting, salmon, tailor, bream | Fish the gutters at first/last light; in the recent winter report, <cite index="5-215,5-216,5-217">Palm Beach, Long Reef and Dee Why developed very good structure and produced plenty of whiting in size and numbers, with live worms the best bait.</cite> |

**Sydney best-month cheat-sheet** (land-based): <cite index="1-242">Bream Jan–May, Dusky Flathead Nov–Apr, Whiting Oct–Apr, Tailor Mar–Aug, Australian Salmon Mar–Jun, Snapper May–Sep, Jewfish/Mulloway Oct–May, Yellowtail Kingfish Nov–May, Luderick Mar–Sep.</cite>

### 2) Pittwater, Hawkesbury River & Broken Bay
The northern Sydney / lower-Hawkesbury system is a big-fish factory. <cite index="5-14,5-16">The Hawkesbury is famous for the mighty jewfish (mulloway); the lower river also produces kingfish, Australian salmon, bream, flathead, luderick, hairtail, crabs and squid.</cite> <cite index="5-21">Pittwater holds schools of kingfish and big squid</cite>, while <cite index="5-18">Broken Bay (around Lion Island) offers reef fishing for bream plus pelagic schools of salmon, tailor and bonito or frigate mackerel.</cite>

- **Hairtail** are a unique cold-season Hawkesbury target — in the May report, <cite index="5-202">the hairtail and jewfish action has been strong in Pittwater and the Hawkesbury</cite>.

| Spot | Type | Target species |
|---|---|---|
| **Hawkesbury River (Brooklyn / Cowan)** | Deep estuary | Mulloway, hairtail (winter), kingfish, flathead, bream |
| **Pittwater (Palm Beach wharf)** | Estuary | Kingfish, squid, salmon |
| **Patonga** | Beach/estuary | Flathead, whiting, bream |
| **Broken Bay / Lion Island** | Reef/bay | Bream, salmon, tailor, bonito |

### 3) Brisbane Water (Woy Woy / Ettalong)
A healthy, protected estuary. <cite index="5-12,5-13">Brisbane Water has active oyster leases that attract big bream and whiting and sand banks perfect for huge flathead; in winter luderick (blackfish) are plentiful, and with no trawlers or crab traps allowed it's a healthy fishing ground.</cite>
- **Ettalong sand flats:** <cite index="3-177,3-178">shallow, calm and perfect for whiting, bream and flathead — the changing tides bring fish in close and the easy access makes it great (and family-friendly).</cite>

### 4) Central Coast lakes & headlands (The Entrance → Tuggerah → Munmorah)
- **The Entrance channel:** <cite index="3-152,3-154">a rapid flow of water connecting the lake to the ocean, a thoroughfare for all kinds of fish — blackfish, bream, flathead and whiting</cite>. Classic **pressure water**; fish the tide changes.
- **The Rip Bridge (Blackwall):** strong-tide deep water. <cite index="3-76,3-77">It produces sizable Australian salmon, mulloway, snapper and kingfish from its deep water, plus tailor and bream in the shallower areas near the bridge.</cite>
- **Tuggerah Lake (Long Jetty / Watkins Jetty):** big shallow lake. Catch logs rank it for <cite index="3-6">dusky flathead, surf bream and bluefish (tailor)</cite>; the FishAngler data shows <cite index="3-34,3-35">dusky flathead and yellowfin bream as the most-caught species.</cite>
- **Budgewoi Lake:** <cite index="3-56">a productive Central Coast lake</cite> for bream and flathead.
- **Lake Munmorah:** <cite index="3-165">a scenic lake where you can catch flathead, bream, trevally, tailor and even Australian salmon.</cite>
- **Terrigal (The Skillion / Haven):** rock and headland. <cite index="3-176">Plenty of impressive kingfish, salmon and tailor to be caught</cite> (and the occasional pelagic surprise). Rock-fishing risk applies.
- **Winney Bay (Copacabana):** <cite index="3-181,3-184">a cracking but off-the-beaten-track rock-fishing spot — access via a trail near the Cape Three Points water tank — home to a variety of species, but rock fishing can be risky so check conditions and wear the right gear.</cite>

### 5) 🔥 The warm-water belt (Wyee Bay / Mannering Park / Myuna Bay)
The southern Lake Macquarie bays next to the Vales Point and Eraring outlets are the headline **hot-water** zone. The warmth shifts the fish community — research notes more <cite index="2-72">tarwhine, silver biddy, bream and southern butterfish</cite> around the thermal influence. Fish the **open bay** near the warm plume (e.g. Mannering Park foreshore, Wyee Bay), **never the closed canals**, and re-check the 1 May–31 Aug night-closure signage above.

### 6) Lake Macquarie (Australia's largest saltwater lake)
<cite index="4-3,4-4">Lake Macquarie covers 110 km² of sheltered water connected to the Pacific through Swansea Channel; ocean species push through on the tide while resident fish thrive in the warm, food-rich shallows year-round.</cite>

| Spot | Type | Target species | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Swansea Channel** | Channel / training walls | Kingfish, mulloway, salmon, trevally, luderick | Premier **pressure water** — <cite index="4-6,4-7,4-9">kings ambush bait in the current, mulloway hold the deeper holes, and the channel training walls produce luderick through winter on cabbage weed.</cite> |
| **Marks Point / Shingle Splitters flats** | Sand flats | Flathead, whiting | <cite index="4-11">Prime flathead and whiting territory you can wade in summer.</cite> |
| **Pulbah Island** | Reef edges | Snapper, trevally | <cite index="4-12">A nature reserve surrounded by reef edges where snapper and trevally feed at first light.</cite> |
| **Croudace Bay / Wangi Wangi / Murrays Beach** | Sheltered bays | Bream, flathead | <cite index="4-10">Sheltered bays offering reliable bream and flathead fishing protected from almost any wind.</cite> |

### 7) Newcastle (the finish line)
<cite index="6-4,6-5">Newcastle Harbour is one of the most productive urban fisheries on the NSW coast — the Hunter River pushes nutrients into a deep channel from Carrington Bridge to the harbour entrance, supporting everything from bream around the wharves to mulloway under the commercial berths at Queens Wharf.</cite>

| Spot | Type | Target species | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nobbys Breakwall** | Breakwall / rock | Kingfish, bonito, tailor, salmon | <cite index="6-6,6-7">Where the channel current meets the open ocean is big-fish territory — kingfish, bonito, tailor and salmon patrol the walls, especially late spring–autumn when water climbs above 20°C.</cite> |
| **Stockton Breakwall** | Breakwall | Mulloway, bream, tailor | Harbour-mouth current and structure. |
| **Queens Wharf / Honeysuckle** | Urban wharf | Mulloway, bream | <cite index="6-5">Mulloway lurk under the commercial berths; bream hold around the wharves.</cite> |
| **Stockton Beach** | Beach (30 km) | Whiting, tailor | <cite index="6-8,6-9">30 km of unbroken sand — the gutters and wash zones are prime whiting and tailor country.</cite> |
| **Bar Beach / Merewether** | Beach | Australian salmon, tailor | <cite index="6-10">South of the harbour these beaches offer reliable salmon fishing when gutters form on a moderate swell.</cite> |

## Quick-reference: what's biting where (winter focus — you're reading this in June)

- **Right now (winter):** Tailor, Australian salmon, luderick, snapper, bream (moving to coast to spawn), jewfish/mulloway in the Hawkesbury & harbour, hairtail in the Hawkesbury, plus the **warm-water bream/flathead edge** in southern Lake Macquarie.
- **Summer (Dec–Feb):** Whiting on the flats, dusky flathead everywhere, kingfish on the channels and walls, squid, trevally.
- **Always worth a shot:** Bream and flathead in every estuary listed.

## Golden rules baked into SendIt

1. Fish **moving water** (run-in/run-out, tide changes) over slack.
2. Hunt **low light** (dawn, dusk, night for jew/mulloway).
3. In winter, chase **warmth** (thermal bays, deeper holes) — but obey canal closures.
4. A **falling barometer** ahead of a front is prime; a high, bluebird day is tough.
5. **Match the bait** that's already there (nippers, prawns, worms, squid, pillies).
6. **Safety beats the catch** — swell + rocks + breakwalls demand respect, every time.

### App data & resilience (v1.4–v1.7)
The app's live data is **not** WillyWeather (that's a paid, key-gated service). SendIt uses free, no-key sources, now split into **independent modules fetched in parallel** so one failing never breaks the others:
- **Weather + wind** (`getForecast`): Open-Meteo (primary) → **MET Norway** (automatic backup) → cached last-good. Switchable in **Settings → Weather source** (Auto / Open-Meteo / MET), plus a manual **Fetch now** button.
- **Tides** (`getTides`): **live-first, per searched location** — Open-Meteo Marine (`cell_selection=sea`) → **Bureau of Meteorology** (via the free, no-key austides proxy) → **TideTurtle** — with live results **cached for days, keyed per location**. When none are reachable, an **on-device harmonic tide engine** takes over: it synthesises the tide from the four principal constituents (M2/S2/K1/O1) anchored to the real Moon/Sun positions and scaled to Sydney/Fort Denison tidal planes — giving real **metre heights**, correct **spring/neap** (tied to moon phase) and diurnal inequality, **valid across the NSW coast (±~0.2 m per BOM/MHL) for decades with zero network**. Heights (current + every High/Low) are shown on the card, the 48-hour table and the chart. Always check the Bureau of Meteorology before a low-tide rock or breakwall session.
  - **Why harbours behaved differently (v2.6 fix):** Open-Meteo Marine is an **8 km, ocean-only** grid that is *"unreliable inland"* (Open-Meteo's own caveat), so estuary/harbour spots — **Newcastle, Wollongong, Swansea, Lake Macquarie** — have *no usable marine cell* and can only get real data from **BOM/austides**, which covers NSW standard ports. austides serves a station list plus per-date high/low (BOM publishes ~7 days ahead only). The station list is now **pre-warmed at boot** and BOM gets a realistic time budget, so these spots reach **live** data instead of silently dropping to the engine. The fallback order is explicit: **live → cached-live (same location) → on-device engine → neutral estimate**, and the source chip + `?debug=true` panel always state which one is in use.
- **Swell / sea temp** (`getSwell`): Open-Meteo Marine; omitted gracefully if unavailable.
- Every request has a 12 s timeout + 2 retries. **Settings → Connection test** pings every endpoint and reports ✓/✗ + latency, so you can see exactly what a corporate firewall or network is blocking.
- **Tide display (v2.4):** the Tides card shows a **Live / Cached / Offline-backup** source label + **confidence**, a **Now / Next** summary, a **48-hour ↔ 7-day** High/Low table with **metre heights**, and clear disclosure when the on-device harmonic **backup engine** is used (with a Retry-live button). Order: live → cached → backup engine → estimate — the backup engine is never presented as official.
- **Finding a spot (v1.6):** the Spots tab supports **search by name or species** (e.g. "Bundeena", "kingfish") alongside the region filter, with a live result count — so all 113 spots stay easy to navigate.
