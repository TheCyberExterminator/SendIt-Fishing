# SendIt Fishing — Rig & Knot Setup Guide

Practical, Australian-east-coast rig setups — organised **by target species** and **by location type**, so you can rig for the fish *or* rig for the spot. Line weights are a starting point; scale up around heavy structure, down in clear/calm water.

> **Universal kit:** a light combo (7 ft, 2–4 kg, 2500 reel, 6–10 lb braid) covers bream/whiting/flathead; a medium-heavy combo (8–9 ft, 4–8 kg, 4000–6000 reel, 15–30 lb braid) covers tailor/salmon/jew/snapper/kings. A spool of **fluorocarbon leader** (6–60 lb depending on target) and a packet of quality chemically-sharpened hooks are non-negotiable.

---

## Part A — Rigs by species

### 🐟 Bream
- **Rig:** Light **running sinker** (ball sinker running straight to the hook, or to a small swivel + 30–50 cm leader). Unweighted in shallow/clear water.
- **Line/leader:** 4–8 lb braid, 6–8 lb fluoro leader.
- **Hook:** #2–1/0 suicide/octopus.
- **Bait/lure:** Live nippers (yabbies), peeled/live prawn, mullet strip, bread; or 2–3" soft plastics & small hardbody lures around structure.
- **How:** Berley lightly; fish the **run-out** around oyster racks, pylons, snags, rock walls. Light = more bites.

### 🐠 Dusky Flathead
- **Rig:** **Jighead + soft plastic** (1/8–3/8 oz, 3–4" paddletail/jerk shad) is the gun method; or a **paternoster/running sinker** with a strip bait or whole pilchard on the bottom.
- **Line/leader:** 6–10 lb braid, 10–15 lb leader (their raspy mouth chafes light leader).
- **Hook:** 1/0–4/0 (size to bait/plastic).
- **How:** Slow-hop along the bottom over sand flats and drop-offs on the **run-out**. Flatties ambush — let it sink, lift, drop.

### 🐟 Sand Whiting
- **Rig:** Light **running ball sinker** to a long-shank hook; or a paternoster for distance off the beach. Surface poppers on warm mornings.
- **Line/leader:** 4–6 lb, 4–6 lb leader.
- **Hook:** #4–#6 long-shank.
- **Bait:** Live beach/blood worms (best), pipis, nippers.
- **How:** **Run-in** over clean sand; keep moving — whiting feed in patches.

### 🐡 Jewfish / Mulloway
- **Rig:** **Running sinker** (or running ball) to a swivel, then heavy leader to a single big hook — or **snell two hooks** for live/large baits. Soft vibes & big plastics work in estuaries.
- **Line/leader:** 15–30 lb braid (estuary) up to 50 lb (beach/rock); **40–60 lb fluoro leader**.
- **Hook:** 5/0–8/0 octopus or circle (circle for self-hooking on the soak).
- **Bait:** Live mullet/yakka, squid (whole or head), fresh fish fillet, live tailor for big models.
- **How:** Deep holes, bridge lights, rock walls and beach gutters; fish the **tide change**, after dark, best around **new/full moon**. Patience game.

### 🐟 Tailor
- **Rig:** **Gang hooks** (3–4 × 4/0 ganged) through a whole pilchard or garfish, often under a float in the wash; or cast metal slugs.
- **Line/leader:** 10–20 lb; **short wire or heavy mono trace** — tailor have shears for teeth.
- **How:** First & last light in the beach gutters and off the rocks; retrieve metals fast.

### 🐟 Australian Salmon
- **Rig:** **Metal slug** (20–40 g) straight through for distance casting; or ganged pilchard under a float.
- **Line/leader:** 10–15 lb, 15–20 lb leader.
- **How:** Look for birds working bait in the surf gutters and off the rocks; hard-fighting winter schools.

### 🐠 Snapper
- **Rig:** **Paternoster** (two dropper loops) for bait on the bottom, or **running sinker** to float a bait back unweighted in current; 5–7" soft plastics on a jighead over reef.
- **Line/leader:** 15–30 lb braid, 30–40 lb leader.
- **Hook:** 5/0–7/0 octopus.
- **Bait:** Whole squid, pilchard, fresh slimy mackerel, octopus.
- **How:** Low-light bite over reef/gravel; berley a trail and drift baits down.

### 🐟 Silver Trevally
- **Rig:** Light **running sinker** or unweighted; berley essential.
- **Line/leader:** 8–12 lb, 8–12 lb leader.
- **Hook:** #1–1/0.
- **Bait/lure:** Peeled prawn, cut pilchard, small soft plastics & metals.
- **How:** Deep estuary holes and jetty/wharf pylons on a **building tide**. Hard pullers for their size.

### 🐋 Yellowtail Kingfish
- **Rig:** **Heavy live-bait rig** — running sinker or balloon float to 60–80 lb leader and a 7/0–9/0 live-bait hook through a live squid/yakka; or **knife/speed jigs** with assist hooks; stickbaits on top.
- **Line/leader:** 30–50 lb braid, **60–80 lb leader** (they bust you on structure).
- **How:** Reefs, headlands, channels and FADs in **strong current**. Lock the drag and turn their head early — kings dive for the reef.

## Part B — Rigs by location type (rig for the spot)

| Location | Go-to rig | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Estuary flats (sand)** | 1/8–1/4 oz jighead + 3" plastic; or light running sinker + nipper | Spooky shallow fish; finesse and stealth win. Flathead, whiting, bream. |
| **Estuary structure (racks, pylons, snags)** | Unweighted/lightly weighted bait or hardbody, heavier 10–12 lb leader | Fish hold tight to cover; you need abrasion resistance to pull them out. |
| **Deep estuary holes & bridges** | Running sinker / soft vibe; up the leader to 30–40 lb | Jew, big flathead, trevally sit deep in the **pressure water**; fish the tide change. |
| **Channel / training walls (e.g. Swansea, The Entrance)** | Live bait under float for kings; paternoster on bottom for jew; float + cabbage weed for luderick | Current funnels bait — present in the flow, not the slack. |
| **Surf beach gutters** | Paternoster or running sinker (snapper lead 2–4 oz) for distance; ganged pillie for tailor/salmon | Hold bottom in the wash; reach the gutter behind the bank. Whiting, bream, tailor, salmon, jew. |
| **Rock platforms & headlands** | Float-fished pilchard/cabbage; metal for spinning; heavy running sinker for deep ledges | Versatile for tailor, drummer, luderick, salmon, kings. **Swell safety is non-negotiable.** |
| **Breakwalls (Nobbys/Stockton)** | Live bait/metal for kings & tailor at the mouth; bait on bottom for mulloway/bream | Harbour-mouth current = big-fish lane. Watch your footing on wet rock. |
| **🔥 Warm-water bays (winter)** | Standard bream/flathead light rigs — fish are just more active | Thermal lift keeps fish feeding; finesse still applies. **Obey canal closures (1 May–31 Aug, 6pm–6am).** |
| **Lake shallows (Tuggerah, Budgewoi, Munmorah)** | Light running sinker + worm/prawn; hardbody/vibe for flathead | Calm, tannin-stained lakes; cover water to find patches. |

## Part C — Knots that hold (learn these four)

1. **FG knot** — braid-to-leader. The slimmest, strongest braid-leader join; passes through guides cleanly for casting. (Practice it at home — it's the one that matters most.)
   - *Quick alternative:* **Double Uni** if you can't tie FG streamside.
2. **Improved Clinch / Uni knot** — leader-to-hook/lure/swivel. Wet it, snug it slowly.
3. **Loctite/loop for lures:** a small **loop knot** (e.g. Lefty's loop) gives hardbodies and plastics free movement = more bites.
4. **Snell** — for running two hooks on big live/dead baits (jew, mulloway). Keeps both hooks inline along the bait.

**Always:** wet every knot before pulling tight (friction = heat = weak line), trim tags close, and check your leader for nicks after every fish — especially flathead and jew.

## Part D — Match-the-conditions (how SendIt picks your rig)

The app recommends a rig by blending:
- **Target species** (from the live probability ranking),
- **Location type** (curated spot or your search/GPS point),
- **Tide & current** (more flow → heavier sinker; finesse on the flats),
- **Wind/swell** (heavier to hold bottom and cast into wind).

So when SendIt says *"Swansea Channel, kings firing"* it'll point you at a **live-bait-under-float, 60–80 lb leader** setup — and when it says *"Tuggerah flats, flathead"* it'll point you at a **1/4 oz jighead and a 4" plastic**. Rig for what's biting, where you are.

> **Tide input (v2.6):** the **tide & current** signal feeding this rig logic is now **live-first per searched location** — real BOM/marine tide where it's reachable (incl. harbour/estuary spots like Newcastle, Wollongong, Swansea, Lake Macquarie), falling back to the on-device harmonic engine only when live data isn't available, and the source is always labelled in-app. So the run-in/run-out timing behind a rig call reflects live tide where there's coverage. Still check the Bureau of Meteorology before any low rock or breakwall session.
